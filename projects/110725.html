Robot Interactive Workshop Development

Developed for: Robotics & Mechatronics Department - Swinburne University of Technology Sarawak.

Session: 4–5 hour

Guide secondary school students to assemble and program a multifunctional robot, gaining hands-on experience in hardware interfacing, computer vision, voice recognition, and AI chatbot integration.

Objectives

  • Introduce core principles of robotics and mechatronics engineering
  • Enable real-time programming and hardware control using Python and Arduino
  • Demonstrate AI-powered features: face tracking, speech recognition, and chatbot interaction
  • Inspire continued interest in STEM and engineering study paths

Core Components

  • Microcontroller: Arduino Uno R3
  • Actuators: Servo motors
  • Vision: Camera module / external webcam
  • Mechanical Parts: Custom 3D-printed designs

Key Functionalities

  • Face Tracking: Real-time detection and tracking with CVZone
  • Speech Recognition: Offline voice commands via VOSK
  • AI Chatbot: Conversational responses powered by the Gemini API
  • Hardware Interface: Python ↔ Arduino control using pyFirmata
  • Customisable Voice Commands: Tailored triggers for interactive behaviors

Outcome

This workshop served as both an educational tool and a promotional initiative to highlight the interdisciplinary potential of robotics and mechatronics engineering.

  • Fully functional Python-based robot with voice control and face tracking
  • Exposure to real-world applications of computer vision, speech recognition, and AI
  • Enhanced student engagement through interactive and creative problem-solving
